The Atomic Age and J. Robert Oppenheimer

J. Robert Oppenheimer, a key figure in the Manhattan Project during World War II, played an integral role in the development of the atomic bomb. Renowned as a brilliant physicist, he led the team that successfully detonated the first nuclear weapon in 1945.

Following the war, Oppenheimer became a vocal advocate for nuclear regulation. He opposed the development of the hydrogen bomb, emphasizing the need for international control over nuclear power. This stance highlighted his commitment to ethical scientific practices in the post-war era.

However, during the Red Scare, Oppenheimer faced severe accusations of communist sympathies. This led to a controversial public hearing that stripped him of his security clearance, tarnishing his reputation. Despite these challenges, Oppenheimer's contributions to physics and his influence on nuclear policy remained significant until his death in 1967.
